# Rhone synthetic case

This is the syntetic case of the Rhône case from Lagnieu to Jons with the Ain tributary confluence donwstream.

Geometry values are estimated from the real model. Median values of width and elevation were estimated for the Rhone and Ain separately to get a value of riverbed and left and right banks. 

Concerning the slope, it was estimated simulating with the real model a high discharge event. Energy slope, friction slope and riverbed slope were considered quite similar in high dischage event to estimate a mean value, which will be considered as constant along to the Rhône and Ain tributary.

## Description of the synthetic geometry:

Due to median values, a multiplicative factor of 10% is added to all values and they were been arrounded, except the slope.

### Ain:
- So_Ain: 0.0008852672
- dz_cross_section = 3.907 m (thalweg to elevation of the banks)
- width_rg = 77.01 m
- width_riverbed = 61.045 m 
- width_rd = 113.071 m

### Rhone:

- So_Rhône: 0.0004131376
- dz_cross_section = 7.151 m (thalweg to elevation of the banks)
- width_rg = 16.48 m
- width_riverbed = 75.135 m 
- width_rd = 126.371 m

## Boundary conditions (referenced values):

- LAG (Rhône): https://hydro.eaufrance.fr/stationhydro/V163002002/synthese -> event 1: Q(t)(Qmj minimum) = 143 m3/s and event 2: Q(Non-exceedance frequency<0.1) = 219 m3/s
- PCH (AIN): https://hydro.eaufrance.fr/stationhydro/V294201001/synthese -> event 1: Q(t)(Qmj minimum) = 4.4 m3/s and event 2: Q(Non-exceedance frequency<0.1) =  20 m3/s
- JNS (Aval): automatic Q(Z) 

## Distribution of reaches:

Model is divided in three reaches: Rhone (LAG - ANT), AIN (PCH-ANT) and Rhone after confluence (ANT-JNS). 

The raw cross sections given at the model are at the extremes of the reaches, all cross sections in the middle of a reach have been interpolated. 

# FEEDBACK

This synthetic case make me realize these points:

- To run interpolation tool of PamHyr2, it seems that in the geometry tab, the two cross sections must be ordered from downstream to upstream, if not, any interpolation is done.
- First cross section in geometry tab must be upstream !
